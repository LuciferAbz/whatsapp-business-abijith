My Thinking

How I approached this task

5 short answers · Performance Marketing Task · MalCare

Q1. Which part did you spend the most time on and why?
The hero and the Problem section. Both are where you either earn the scroll or lose it — the hero has to match the ad, and the problem section has to make the reader feel seen before they'll trust anything else on the page.
Q2. Which section are you least confident about?
The benefits section. Three identical cards on mobile tend to lose the reader by the third one, and I'd want to give the auto-replies card more visual weight since that's the feature that actually makes this audience act.
Q3. If conversion is poor, what is the first thing you would try?
Test the headline first — pit the current version against something outcome-first like "Get More Orders on WhatsApp, Starting Today." Most bounces happen in the first few seconds, so that's where I'd look before changing anything else.
Q4. What did you intentionally leave out even though it's common on landing pages?
Navigation, comparison tables, and social share buttons. All three pull attention away from the one action I want the user to take, and on cold traffic that's a conversion you don't get back.
Q5. Which skill was hardest here: copy, structure, or UX?
Copy. This audience needs short sentences, zero jargon, and an emotional angle that lands through PAS — and the Hindi-English in the testimonials was a deliberate choice to reflect how they actually talk, not just translate it.
